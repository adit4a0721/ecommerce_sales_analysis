#  E-Commerce Sales Analysis using SQL

##  Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing an **e-commerce sales database** using **SQL** to extract meaningful business insights.  
The goal is to understand **customer behavior, sales performance, product trends, and revenue growth** by solving real-world business questions commonly asked in Data Analyst interviews.

This project simulates how data analysts work with transactional data in real companies.

---

##  Database Schema
The analysis is based on the following tables:

- **customers** – customer details and city information  
- **products** – product names, categories, and prices  
- **orders** – order dates and order amounts  
- **order_items** – quantity of products sold per order  
- **payments** – payment method and payment status  

---

##  Business Questions Solved

###  Basic Analysis
- Total number of customers  
- Total revenue generated  
- Number of orders per city  

###  Intermediate Analysis
- Monthly revenue trend  
- Top 5 customers by total spend  
- Most sold products  
- Average order value  
- Orders by payment method  

###  Advanced Analysis
- Repeat vs one-time customers  
- Customers who never placed an order  
- Category-wise revenue contribution  
- Failed payment orders  
- Month-over-month revenue growth  
- Top 3 best-selling products in each category  

---

##  SQL Concepts Used
- INNER JOIN, LEFT JOIN  
- GROUP BY and HAVING  
- Aggregate functions (SUM, COUNT, AVG)  
- Subqueries  
- Window functions (RANK, LAG)  
- Date functions for time-based analysis  

---

##  Key Insights
- Electronics category generates the highest revenue  
- Repeat customers contribute significantly more revenue than one-time customers  
- Certain products dominate sales within each category  
- Monthly revenue shows noticeable growth trends  
- UPI and Card are the most commonly used payment methods
